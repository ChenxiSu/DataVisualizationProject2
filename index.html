A Closer Look into the Effects of Higher Temperatures

A rise in worldwide temperatures leads to an increase in carbon dioxide in the atmosphere, higher sea levels, and a decrease in sea ice extent. Drag the slider to watch how the polar bear copes as sea level rises, CO2 concentration increases, and sea ice shrinks.The impacts of a dwindling ice cover in the Arctic are far-reaching, from species endangerment to enhanced global warming, to the weakening or shut-down of global ocean circulation.

"Taken as a whole, the range of published evidence indicates that the net damage costs of climate change are likely to be significant and to increase over time."-Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change"
